# EAP Students' AI Disclosure Intention

**arXiv:** 2604.10991v1 | **Published:** 2026-04-13 | **Cats:** cs.HC, cs.AI
**Authors:** Yiran Du, Huimin He
**PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.10991v1

## Abstract

The growing use of generative artificial intelligence (AI) in academic writing has raised increasing concerns regarding transparency and academic integrity in higher education. This study examines the psychological factors influencing English for Academic Purposes (EAP) students' intention to disclose their use of AI tools. Drawing on the cognition-affect-conation (CAC) framework, the study proposes a model integrating both enabling and inhibiting factors shaping disclosure intention. A sequential explanatory mixed-methods design was employed with 324 EAP students at an English-medium instruction university in China.

## Key Findings

| Factor | Effect on Disclosure Intention |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| 心理安全感 (psychological safety) | ↑ 促进披露 |
| 恐惧负面评价 (fear of negative evaluation) | ↓ 抑制披露 |

**定性发现:**
- 支持性教师实践 + 明确指导 → 提升心理安全感
- 政策模糊性 + 名誉担忧 → 加剧恐惧感，抑制披露

## Practical Implications

1. 制定**清晰的 AI 使用政策**
2. **去污名化**合理的 AI 辅助行为
3. 营造**支持性的教学环境**

## Disclosure vs. Concealment

与另一篇 AI学术诚信与隐瞒行为 互补：披露意愿和隐瞒意愿不是同一维度的两端，而是独立的构念。
