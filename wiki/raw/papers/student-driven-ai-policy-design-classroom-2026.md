# Student-Driven AI Policy in Design Classroom

**arXiv:** 2604.10851v1 | **Published:** 2026-04-12 | **Cats:** cs.HC
**Authors:** Kaoru Seki, Manisha Vijay, Yasmine Kotturi
**PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.10851v1

## Abstract

Generative AI is reshaping education, yet most university AI policies are written without students and focus on penalizing misuse. This top-down approach sidelines those most affected from decisions that shape their everyday learning. We examine how participatory, student-driven AI policy design can address this disconnect. We report on a three-part workshop series in a graduate design course at a minority-serving university in the U.S., where two student leaders facilitated discussions without faculty present.

## Key Findings

学生自发形成的 AI 政策揭示了**顶层设计中被忽视的问题**：

1. **双重标准问题:** 要求学生披露或禁用 AI，但教师不受同等约束
2. **参与价值超越政策本身:** 学生参与 AI 治理本身就有教育意义
3. **"叫学生进来"而非"叫学生出去"** 的策略可迁移到其他学科

## Workshop 模型

- 三部分工作坊
- 学生主导（无教师在场）
- 共同撰写10条政策建议
- 以 zine 形式在校园传播

## 核心洞察

> 将学生纳入 AI 治理的价值不仅在于最终的政策文本，更在于参与过程本身培养的批判性思维和治理能力。
